Slide 2 of 6 · Overview

Persistent, threaded messaging
integrated across your M365 workspace.

🏢
Teams Chat provides persistent, searchable messaging scoped to individuals, ad-hoc groups, or structured team channels. Unlike email, conversations are threaded, context is preserved, and files are co-located with the discussion that originated them.

Two kinds of chat

💬

Direct Message (DM)

Private, persistent conversation between one or more individuals. Visible only to participants; ideal for focused discussion outside a channel context.

📣

Channel Post

A message posted to a shared team channel. Visible to all channel members; supports threaded replies and is the appropriate venue for team-wide communication.

Teams Chat vs. Email

  • ⚡

    Lower friction

    No subject lines, signatures, or threading overhead. Ideal for quick questions, status updates, and decisions that don't warrant a formal email.

  • 🔍

    Persistent, searchable history

    All messages are indexed and retrievable via search. Prior decisions, shared files, and context remain accessible without digging through email archives.

  • 🧵

    Threaded conversations

    Replies nest under the originating message, keeping related discussion contained and readable without fragmenting across inboxes.

    • Slide 3 of 6 · Getting Started

    Initiating a conversation —
    Direct Message or Channel.

    Send a Direct Message

    1. 1

      Select Chat in the left navigation rail

      Your recent conversations are listed here in reverse chronological order.

    2. 2

      Click the compose (✏️) icon or + New Chat

      A recipient field appears at the top of the conversation pane.

    3. 3

      Enter the recipient's name and compose your message

      Directory suggestions appear as you type. Select the recipient, enter your message, and press Enter to send.

    Post in a Channel

    1. 1

      Select Teams in the left navigation rail

      Expand your team (e.g., DOI SHIP, IT Department) to reveal its channels.

    2. 2

      Select the appropriate channel

      General serves as the default; purpose-specific channels exist for focused topics and workstreams.

    3. 3

      Click New Conversation and compose

      Add a subject line for announcements or substantive posts — it aids searchability and sets context at a glance.

    Note: Always use Reply to respond within an existing thread rather than starting a new post. This keeps related content grouped and preserves conversation context.

    Slide 4 of 6 · Key Features

    Core capabilities
    worth knowing well.

    @

    @Mention someone

    Type @Name to trigger a targeted notification for that individual regardless of their current activity status. Use @channel to notify all channel members.

    😄

    React with emojis

    Hover over a message and select a reaction to acknowledge without generating a reply. Reduces notification noise for the recipient and keeps threads uncluttered.

    📎

    Share files

    Attach files via the 📎 icon or paste a SharePoint/OneDrive link. Files are co-located with the conversation, versioned, and accessible to all participants.

    🧵

    Reply in thread

    In channels, always use Reply — not a new post. Keeps conversations together and easy to follow.

    🔔

    Mute notifications

    Right-click any chat or channel and select Mute to suppress notifications without leaving the conversation. Useful during deep-focus work sessions.

    ⭐

    Pin important chats

    Right-click a conversation and select Pin to anchor it at the top of your chat list for fast access to high-priority threads.

    Slide 5 of 6 · Hands-On Practice

    Put it to use —
    starting today.

    🏋️

    Hands-on exercises to reinforce these workflows

    Complete these exercises to reinforce the key workflows covered in this guide:

    Challenge 1

    Initiate a Direct Message

    Open a new DM to any colleague. A brief test message is sufficient — the goal is to confirm the send workflow.

    Challenge 2

    React to a message

    Hover over any channel message and apply a reaction. Note that reactions replace low-value acknowledgment replies and keep threads clean.

    Challenge 3

    @Mention someone

    In a channel reply, type @ followed by a colleague's name to trigger a targeted notification, regardless of their current notification settings.

    Challenge 4

    Pin a conversation

    Right-click a high-frequency conversation and select Pin to surface it at the top of your chat list permanently.

    Challenge 5

    Search for something

    Use Ctrl+E to search across messages, people, and files. Confirm that historical content is retrievable by searching a past topic.

    Challenge 6

    Share a file

    Attach a file to any chat via the paperclip icon and confirm the recipient can access it. Files route through SharePoint automatically.

    💡 Good to Know

    Presence status

    Set your status to Do Not Disturb during focused work periods. Incoming messages are held without notification and remain accessible when you return.

    💡 Good to Know

    Edit & delete

    Select the ··· menu on any sent message to edit content or delete it entirely. Edits are timestamped and visible to participants.

    💡 Good to Know

    Rich text formatting

    The formatting toolbar (A icon) supports bold, lists, code blocks, and inline tables — useful for structured updates and technical content.
